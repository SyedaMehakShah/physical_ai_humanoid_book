## Academic Literature

### Foundational Papers

**Siciliano, B., & Khatib, O. (Eds.). (2016). Springer Handbook of Robotics.**
- Comprehensive reference covering all aspects of robotics
- Essential reading for understanding the breadth of robotics research
- Covers kinematics, dynamics, control, perception, and applications

**Kajita, S., Kanehiro, F., Kaneko, K., Yokoi, K., & Hirukawa, H. (2003). The 3D Linear Inverted Pendulum Mode: A simple modeling for a biped walking pattern generation.**
- Fundamental paper on bipedal walking control
- Introduces the Linear Inverted Pendulum Mode (LIPM) for walking pattern generation
- Key reference for humanoid balance control

**Khatib, O. (1987). A unified approach for motion and force control of robot manipulators: The operational space formulation.**
- Introduces operational space control
- Fundamental for understanding whole-body control in humanoid robots
- Essential for manipulation and interaction control

**Sardain, P., & Bessonnet, G. (2004). Forces acting on a biped robot. Center of pressure-zero moment point.**
- Explains ZMP (Zero-Moment Point) control for bipedal robots
- Critical for understanding balance and walking control
- Mathematical foundation for stable humanoid locomotion

**Schaal, S. (1999). Is imitation learning the route to humanoid robots?**
- Explores imitation learning as a path to humanoid robotics
- Discusses learning from human demonstrations
- Foundational for robot learning and human-robot interaction

### Recent Advances

**Hwangbo, J., Lee, J., Dosovitskiy, A., Bellicoso, C., Tsounis, V., Koltun, V., & Hutter, M. (2019). Learning agile and dynamic motor skills for legged robots. Science Robotics, 4(26).**
- Demonstrates learning-based control for dynamic locomotion
- Shows how machine learning can improve robot agility
- Important for modern approaches to humanoid control

**Rajeswaran, A., Kumar, V., Gupta, A., & Finn, C. (2021). A game theoretic framework for model based imitation learning. International Conference on Machine Learning.**
- Advanced approach to imitation learning
- Relevant for humanoid robots learning from human demonstrations
- Modern approach combining game theory with machine learning

**Menda, S., Driggs-Campbell, K., Phan-Minh, T., Chen, R., Kochenderfer, M. J., & Boyd, S. (2019). Humanoid robot locomotion using model predictive control. IEEE/RSJ International Conference on Intelligent Robots and Systems.**
- Modern approach to humanoid locomotion control
- Uses Model Predictive Control (MPC) for walking
- Shows integration of optimization with control

## Books and Textbooks

### Core Robotics Textbooks

**"Introduction to Robotics: Mechanics and Control" by John J. Craig**
- Classic textbook covering fundamental robotics concepts
- Excellent for understanding kinematics and dynamics
- Clear explanations with practical examples

**"Robotics: Modelling, Planning and Control" by Bruno Siciliano, Lorenzo Sciavicco, Luigi Villani, and Giuseppe Oriolo**
- Comprehensive coverage of robotics fundamentals
- Strong mathematical foundation
- Good balance of theory and practice

**"Robotics, Vision and Control: Fundamental Algorithms In MATLAB" by Peter Corke**
- Practical approach with MATLAB examples
- Excellent for learning implementation
- Covers both robotics and computer vision

**"Humanoid Robotics: A Reference" by Ambarish Goswami and Prahlad Vadakkepat**

- Covers all aspects from design to control
- Good for understanding the field's breadth
